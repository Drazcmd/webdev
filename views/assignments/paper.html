This assignment is for students enrolled in COMP531.

For this assignment you will write a paper, prepare a presentation, and deliver that presentation the last week of class.

Research

You will investigate a topic and submit it for approval. The topic is fairly open but naturally should be related to web development and/or web design. You could present a detailed study of a new technolgy or aspect of the web, you could compare and contrast two or three technology choices, you could investigate a topic on web security or e-commerce, or how big data is being used today and the potentials for the future. Or you could present a study of user experience in different website designs, or a comparison of how certain design details impact the usability of a website. Or you could research ARIA and tell us all about why we should or should not include ARIA tags. Or you could provide a retrospective on a past leading technology, what it promised, what it delivered, but then how it fell short to technologies that superceded it (e.g., Java Applets, Flash, or Ruby on Rails).

What is important is that your topic is meaningful and allows you to demonstrate your ability to critically think about some topic related to web development or design and convey an analysis of said topic.

For example, you might research about upcoming new technologies such as ReactJS and present a comparison of it with AngularJS. But it needs to be more than a laundry list. You need to demonstrate an understanding of the two technologies, how they are similar, how they are different, and make a case for why we should use one over the other. It's no good just to compare and constrast without some critical understanding and critique.

Do some hunting around and come up with an idea. Present it to me in an email for approval. There will be no duplicate topics, so the sooner you pin yours down the better.

Paper

Once you have been approved, write a 1000 to 2000 word paper on your topic. Pay special attention that the contents of your paper focus on critical analysis and not just describing the topic (we can all read wikipedia). Be sure to proof read your paper. You are invited to have your peers read your papers and give you suggestions and comments, but of course you are doing all of the writing yourself.

Presentation

During the last week of the course you will deliver a 6 minute presentation on your topic. Presumably you will require some presentation materials to facillitate. These may be slides (you might checkout react.js for this), selected web sites, what ever you would like. Whatever presentation materials you develop, submit these along with your paper. If it's just a list of websites, then submit a text file that has these website urls and a short description (1-3 sentences) of what content you will highlight on the sites (i.e., what will you be doing with these sites, what will you say).

Turnin both your paper and your presentation materials.
